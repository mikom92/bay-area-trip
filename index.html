Route, day by day

Seven nights in one base before the bootcamp — SF, wine country and the coast all as day trips — then five company-covered, one buffer night before the flight home.

Sun · 13 SepArrival

Sunnyvale + Palo Alto

Land at SFO 13:50 on LO35. Pick up the SUV, 45-minute drive south to Maple Tree Inn — this is home base for the whole week, no more packing until Sunday. Light evening: a walk through Stanford, dinner in Palo Alto.

Mon · 14 SepPilgrimage

The Silicon Valley Circuit

Computer History Museum, Apple Park, Googleplex — all a short drive from the hotel. The easiest day of the week, back at Maple Tree Inn by dinner. On the way back, Limón in Mountain View does great Peruvian rotisserie chicken and ceviche — right by the CHM.

Tue · 15 SepCoast

Santa Cruz

Boardwalk and Natural Bridges, 40 minutes down the coast. Dreamforce kicks off in the city today; you're nowhere near it — and it never touches your budget, since you're not booking a single SF night.

📍 Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk 📍 Natural Bridges
Wed · 16 SepWine Country

Napa & Sonoma

A weekday visit — thinner crowds, calmer tasting rooms. Longest drive of the "easy" days from Sunnyvale (~3–3.5h total), so plan the day around it. Taste and spit at the tastings — you're driving back.

📍 Napa Valley 📍 Sonoma Valley
Thu · 17 SepDrive Day

Monterey & Big Sur

Highway 1 south to Bixby Bridge and the cliffs — the drive you've been looking forward to, and the big one of the week. Out-and-back from Sunnyvale.

📍 Monterey 📍 Bixby Creek Bridge
Fri · 18 SepCity

Alcatraz + North Beach

Dreamforce cleared out yesterday — quietest day to be downtown all week. Drive up (~45 min), ferry to the island from Pier 33 — Pier 39 is right next door, worth a wander for the sea lions and boardwalk. Coit Tower and North Beach after; dinner at Frascati on Hyde St (cozy Italian, cable cars roll right by) before driving back to Maple Tree Inn.

Book Alcatraz now — the 90-day window is open ↗
📍 Alcatraz Island 📍 Pier 39 📍 Coit Tower 📍 North Beach 📍 Frascati
Sat · 19 SepCoast

Muir Woods + Point Reyes

Redwoods in the morning, cliffs and the lighthouse in the afternoon — the whole northern coast in a single day, and the last night at Maple Tree Inn before the move. You're crossing the Golden Gate Bridge anyway — stop in Sausalito for a coffee or lunch (laid-back, Mediterranean-village vibe, right at the north end of the bridge), and swing by Battery East Trail on the SF side for one of the best bridge views around, no detour required.

Reserve Muir Woods parking / shuttle slot ↗
📍 Muir Woods 📍 Point Reyes 📍 Sausalito 📍 Battery East Trail
Sun · 20 SepTransfer

Move to Redwood City

First and only checkout of the week. Checkout from Maple Tree Inn, ~30-minute drive north to Residence Inn by Marriott Redwood City San Carlos (book via Navan) — company coverage starts today. Suite-style room with a kitchenette, ~4.7 mi from HQ. Donato Enoteca, a few minutes away, is a nice welcome dinner — contemporary Italian, wood-fired pizza, open Sunday evenings.

📍 Residence Inn San Carlos 📍 Donato Enoteca

Before you book

One base, zero relocations Every day before the bootcamp is a round-trip from Maple Tree Inn — SF, wine country, the coast, all day trips. Side effect: since you never book an SF night, Dreamforce (Sep 15–17, ~250,000 attendees at Moscone) can't touch your budget at all. Trade-off: three of the seven days run 2.5–3.5h of driving each way.
Confirmed by the team Residence Inn San Carlos is covered Sun 20 – Thu 24 (5 nights, that bootcamp week) — not just Tue–Thu. Check-in Sunday frees up Monday as a bonus day.
Reserve early Alcatraz (90-day window) and Muir Woods parking both sell out — lock these in first.
Points lever, confirmed ~7,050 Booking points bring the core 7-night Maple Tree Inn stay down to a confirmed 2,475 zł (from 4,444 zł cash) — real price, not an estimate.
Flight is genuinely nonstop LO35/LO37 outbound is the same nonstop WAW–SFO service on a 787 Dreamliner. The Fri 25 return trades that nonstop for a LAX connection — one day earlier home, ~2h45m longer in transit.
Designated driver, wine country edition California's legal limit is 0.08% BAC — but DUI can still apply below that if you're visibly impaired, so there's no real "safe number" while driving. Spit buckets are standard at every Napa/Sonoma tasting room; use them and let your companions enjoy the full pour.
Bootcamp commute ≠ your road trip Book Sun 20 – Thu 24 via Navan — Residence Inn by Marriott Redwood City San Carlos (~4.7 mi from HQ, kitchenette + free breakfast) earns Bonvoy points and replaces CitizenM, which came out over the price cap. "Uber-only" is company policy for the office commute; it doesn't touch your self-paid SUV for Big Sur, Napa or Point Reyes.
